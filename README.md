## What is Ketamine?
Ketamine, often referred to as a dissociative anesthetic, has been used in medicine since the 1960s. It's available in liquid form for injections and as a powder for reconstitution. Medically, it's prescribed for anesthesia, pain management, and increasingly for treatment-resistant depression under supervised conditions like esketamine nasal spray (Spravato).

However, recreational or unregulated use poses significant health risks, including dissociation, hallucinations, bladder damage (known as \ketamine cystitis\ and potential for addiction. In 2026, global regulations remain strict, with ongoing efforts to curb misuse amid rising reports of contaminated street supplies.

### Forms of Ketamine: Liquid vs. Powder
- **Liquid Ketamine**: Typically pharmaceutical-grade vials used in clinical settings. It's clear and sterile, dosed precisely for medical procedures.
- **Powder Ketamine**: Derived from liquid form, this crystalline substance is more common in non-medical contexts. Purity varies widely, often leading to overdoses or adulteration with harmful substances.

Understanding these forms is crucial for anyone researching ketamine, but sourcing must always be through licensed channels.
